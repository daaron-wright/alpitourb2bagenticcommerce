01 — The shift

From four assistants to one orchestrated platform

Today AlpiGPT exposes four specialist agents through a menu. Each reads its own manually-maintained knowledge base and answers in its own lane — there is no orchestration across domains and nothing executes. The target is a Super-Agent that understands a complex request, coordinates the domains, and an agentic platform that completes the commercial action.

Today

Four siloed specialists

  • Pick an agent from a menu — flights, hotels, and two more — one domain at a time.
  • Each agent reads its own knowledge base, maintained by hand in document repositories.
  • Informational only — it answers questions; it does not build, price, or book.
  • No memory of the customer, the quote, or the outcome across domains.
AlpiGPT 2.0

Super-Agent + agentic platform

  • One orchestrator decomposes a complex brief and delegates to domain agents.
  • Action agents execute — assemble a quote, price it, upsell, book, re-book.
  • A policy-as-code layer governs the knowledge base and what every agent may do.
  • Humans stay in the loop where money, margin, or risk crosses a threshold.

05 — The configurer

Policy-as-code as the knowledge-base configurer

Today the knowledge bases are maintained by hand in document repositories — they drift, conflict, and can't be enforced. Policy-as-code turns that maintenance into governed, executable rules: one place that defines what is current, what each agent may read or do, and where a human must step in. It governs every flow above.

One rule set, enforced at runtime — not a binder of PDFs

Instead of trusting that a manually-updated document is current, every agent answer and every action is checked against policy-as-code at the moment it runs. The same rules that configure the knowledge base also bound agent autonomy and define the human gates.

# knowledge currency
rule kb.source.stale when age > ttl[domain] → flag + refresh + suppress_as_fact
# agent autonomy bound
rule discount.beyond_bound → route(human: agent)
# compliance
rule destination.entry_change → propagate(KB) + notify(affected_bookings)

What it configures

  • Knowledge currency & source-of-truth precedence per domain
  • What each agent may read, and what it may write
  • Pricing, discount & commission bounds
  • Where a human gate is mandatory
  • Regional, fare-rule & compliance constraints

Why it matters here

  • The KB stops drifting — currency is enforced, not hoped for
  • Agents can act safely because the bounds are explicit
  • One change propagates to every flow and every channel at once
  • Every answer & action is auditable against the rule that governed it

›Rule store & engine: authoring, versioning, sign-off, runtime evaluation
›KB pipeline: ingestion, currency TTLs, source precedence, embeddings refresh
›Binding: how rules attach to agent read/write scopes

06 — Cross-cutting

Platform primitives every flow relies on

Independent of any single flow, these are the shared surfaces the platform needs. They are the connective tissue that makes the agentic behaviour legible, governable and accountable.

Chat orchestrator

The single conversational surface where the Super-Agent understands, plans and responds. The one place users — agent or traveler — interact with the system.

surface · the Super-Agent

Workflow DAG

A visible plan/trace of what the agents are doing — decompose, delegate, act, merge — so a complex orchestration is legible, not a black box.

explainability · the plan made visible

Human-in-the-loop queue

Where the gated decisions land — discounts beyond bound, refunds, exceptions, cost-bearing re-books. Evidence attached, approve / modify / reject.

user agency · the gate

Execution confirmation

After an action runs, what it did and its blast radius — what was booked, charged, re-issued, and who was notified. The loop closes visibly.

consequence transparency · after the act

Explainability & provenance

Every recommendation cites its sources; every number carries a reason. The grounding contract that keeps the Super-Agent trustworthy.

trust · cited, not asserted

Learning & accountability log

Outcomes and human overrides are captured as teaching signals and as an audit trail — the system improves, and every action stays accountable.

the feedback loop · learns from outcomes
