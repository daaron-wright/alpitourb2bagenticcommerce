01 · The shift

From four assistants to one orchestrated platform

Today

Four siloed specialists

  • Pick an agent from a menu — flights, hotels, and two more — one domain at a time.
  • Each reads its own knowledge base, maintained by hand in document repositories.
  • Informational only — it answers; it does not build, price, or book.
  • No memory of the customer, the quote, or the outcome across domains.
AlpiGPT 2.0

Super-Agent + agentic platform

  • One orchestrator decomposes a complex brief and delegates to domain agents.
  • Action agents execute — assemble a quote, price it, upsell, book, re-book.
  • Policy-as-code governs the knowledge base and what every agent may do.
  • Humans stay in the loop where money, margin, or risk crosses a threshold.

05 · The configurer

Policy-as-code as the knowledge-base configurer

One rule set, enforced at runtime — not a binder of PDFs

Today the knowledge bases are maintained by hand — they drift, conflict, and can't be enforced. Policy-as-code turns that into governed, executable rules: what is current, what each agent may read or do, and where a human steps in. It governs every flow.

# knowledge currency
rule kb.source.stale when age > ttl[domain] → flag + refresh
# autonomy bound
rule discount.beyond_bound → route(human: agent)
# compliance
rule destination.entry_change → propagate(KB) + notify(bookings)

What it configures

  • KB currency & source-of-truth precedence per domain
  • What each agent may read, and may write
  • Pricing, discount & commission bounds
  • Where a human gate is mandatory

Why it matters

  • The KB stops drifting — currency is enforced
  • Agents can act safely — bounds are explicit
  • One change propagates to every flow & channel
  • Every action audits against the rule that governed it
