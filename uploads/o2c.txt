Reimagining the Order-to-Cash (O2C) process is not about incremental workflow optimization. It is about redesigning the entire commercial fulfillment value stream as a digital product ecosystem centered on speed, intelligence, automation, customer experience, and operational resilience.
For a company like Dow, the biggest opportunity is transforming O2C from:
A fragmented ERP transaction chain
Manual coordination across sales, supply chain, logistics, finance, and customer service
Reactive exception handling
Into:
An intelligent, event-driven, AI-enabled revenue execution platform.
What “Reimagined O2C” Looks Like
Traditional O2C:

Core Design Principles
1. Design Around Customer Journeys
Do not organize around internal ERP modules.
Organize around:
Quote-to-order
Configure-to-commit
Deliver-to-promise
Invoice-to-pay
Dispute-to-resolution
Different customers may require different O2C journeys:
Strategic accounts
Spot buyers
Distribution partners
Contract customers
Global accounts
Each journey should become a digital product.

2. Shift From Transaction Processing to Intelligent Orchestration
Traditional O2C:
Humans coordinate across silos
Future O2C:
Platforms orchestrate decisions automatically
Examples:
AI prioritizes constrained inventory
Dynamic ATP recalculates in real time
Logistics rerouting happens automatically
Pricing exceptions are risk-scored
Payment risks predicted before invoicing

3. Make O2C Event-Driven
Traditional:
Batch ERP processing
Reimagined:
Real-time event architecture
Examples:
Customer changes order → inventory recalculated instantly
Plant outage → commitments reprioritized automatically
Late shipment → proactive customer notification
Payment delay → collections workflow triggered automatically
This requires:
APIs
Streaming platforms
Real-time integration
Workflow orchestration

What Usually Breaks O2C Transformation
1. ERP-Centric Thinking
ERP should support orchestration — not define the business process.

2. Functional Silos
Sales, supply chain, logistics, and finance must operate as one value stream.

3. Excessive Customization
Avoid deeply customizing ERP logic.
Instead:
externalize workflows
use orchestration layers
use APIs

4. No Product Ownership
O2C needs persistent ownership, not project teams.

5. Treating Automation as the Goal
The goal is:
better decisions
better customer outcomes
faster revenue realization
Automation is only an enabler.
